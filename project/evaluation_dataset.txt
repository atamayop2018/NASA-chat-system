Q1. What problems did Apollo 13 encounter during its mission?
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
Expected answer (grounded in AS13_PAO / AS13_TEC transcripts):
    Apollo 13 suffered an oxygen tank rupture in the Service Module roughly 56
    hours into the flight. The explosion crippled the SM, forced the crew
    (Lovell, Swigert, Haise) to abandon the planned lunar landing, and required
    them to use the Lunar Module "Aquarius" as a lifeboat. Subsequent challenges
    included rapidly depleting power, water, and oxygen reserves; rising CO2
    levels in the LM that forced the crew to improvise a "mailbox" CO2 scrubber
    adapter; navigation in a damaged spacecraft; and a precise free-return
    trajectory burn around the Moon to bring them home safely.

Expected source mission tag: apollo_13
Expected document categories : public_affairs_officer, technical


--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
Q2. Summarize the key communications during the Apollo 11 lunar descent and
    landing.
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
Expected answer (grounded in a11transcript_tec / a11transcript_cm):
    The transcripts capture the dialogue between Mission Control (CapCom Charlie
    Duke), Command Module pilot Michael Collins in Columbia, and Lunar Module
    pilots Neil Armstrong and Buzz Aldrin in Eagle. Highlights include the 1201
    and 1202 program alarms during powered descent, Armstrong manually steering
    Eagle past a boulder field, the call "Houston, Tranquility Base here. The
    Eagle has landed.", the post-landing stay/no-stay decisions, and Armstrong's
    "That's one small step for [a] man, one giant leap for mankind" upon
    stepping onto the lunar surface.

Expected source mission tag: apollo_11
Expected document categories : technical, command_module

Q3. What were the final voice transmissions from the Challenger STS-51L crew?
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
Expected answer (grounded in 107/108/109-AAG_STS-51L_Mission_Audio_transcript):
    The cockpit/air-to-ground audio captures normal launch callouts up to and
    including "Roger, go at throttle up" from Pilot Mike Smith. Around T+73
    seconds an exclamation of "Uh oh" is recorded shortly before the loss of
    signal caused by the breakup of the vehicle. The recovered voice recorder
    transcripts indicate routine, professional communications with no awareness
    of the impending failure prior to that final exclamation.

Expected source mission tag: challenger
Expected document categories : mission_audio

Q4. According to the Apollo 11 flight plan, what activities were scheduled for
    the EVA on the lunar surface?
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
Expected answer (grounded in Apollo_11_Flight_Plan_HSK_textract):
    The flight plan allotted approximately 2 hours 40 minutes of EVA time and
    scheduled, in order: LM egress and contingency sample collection;
    deployment of the TV camera and S-band antenna; planting of the U.S. flag
    and reading of the plaque; documented sample collection (bulk and
    documented); deployment of the Early Apollo Scientific Experiments Package
    (EASEP) including the passive seismic experiment and the laser ranging
    retro-reflector; collection of core tube and additional rock samples; and
    EVA closeout with sample/equipment ingress back into Eagle.

Expected source mission tag: apollo_11
Expected document categories : flight_plan


--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
Q5. Compare how the public affairs officer narrated Apollo 11 versus
    Apollo 13.
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
Expected answer (grounded in a11transcript_pao + AS13_PAO):
    The Apollo 11 PAO transcript is celebratory and milestone-focused: lunar
    descent commentary, "the Eagle has landed", first step onto the Moon, and
    flag deployment. The Apollo 13 PAO transcript shifts dramatically once the
    oxygen tank fails; it covers the "Houston, we've had a problem" report, the
    move into the LM lifeboat, periodic updates on consumables (power, water,
    LiOH), trajectory burns, and the worldwide attention surrounding the safe
    splashdown. Together they show the PAO role pivoting from triumphant
    narration to crisis communication.

Expected source mission tag: apollo_11 + apollo_13
Expected document categories : public_affairs_officer

Q6. What technical issues were discussed in the Apollo 11 command module
    transcripts?
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
Expected answer (grounded in a11transscript_cm + technical):
    The CM transcripts cover Service Propulsion System (SPS) burns for lunar
    orbit insertion and trans-Earth injection, IMU alignments and platform
    realignments, navigation star checks performed by Collins while solo in
    Columbia, communication checks (S-band high gain antenna, omni antennas),
    fuel cell and cryogenic tank monitoring, and the rendezvous and docking
    sequence with Eagle after lunar liftoff.

Expected source mission tag: apollo_11
Expected document categories : command_module, technical

Expected source mission tag: apollo_11

Q8. (Negative test) Did Apollo 13 successfully land on the Moon?
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
Expected answer:
    No. The system should clearly state that Apollo 13's lunar landing was
    aborted following the Service Module oxygen tank rupture. The mission
    became a survival/return-to-Earth operation; the LM Aquarius was used as a
    lifeboat and the crew safely splashed down without ever landing on the
    lunar surface.

This question primarily tests faithfulness — the model must not hallucinate a
landing that did not occur.


--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
Q9. (Out-of-scope test) What did the Mars Curiosity rover discover?
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
Expected answer:
    The system should respond that the available NASA archives in this
    knowledge base only cover Apollo 11, Apollo 13, and Challenger, and that
    no information about the Mars Curiosity rover is present in the indexed
    documents. It should refuse to fabricate details and recommend a different
    source for that mission.

This question tests both faithfulness and the system prompt's instruction in
[`llm_client.py`](Project-NASA-Mission-Intelligence-Starter/llm_client.py:10) to admit when context is insufficient.

Q10. List the missions covered by this knowledge base and their general topics.
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
Expected answer:
    The indexed corpus covers three NASA missions:
        1. Apollo 11 — first crewed lunar landing (July 1969). Documents include
           PAO, CM, TEC transcripts, the flight plan, and the NTRS archive
           report (19710015566) and the textract document 19900066485.
        2. Apollo 13 — third crewed lunar mission (April 1970), aborted due to
           an oxygen tank explosion. Documents include CM, PAO and TEC
           transcripts.
        3. Challenger STS-51L — Space Shuttle mission ending in vehicle loss
           on January 28, 1986. Documents include audio transcripts 107, 108,
           and 109 from the Air-to-Ground / mission audio.

RAGAS Evaluation Notes
================================================================================
For each question above the chat sidebar should display:
    - nv_response_relevancy        (target ≥ 0.80)
    - faithfulness                 (target ≥ 0.85)
    - bleu_score                   (sanity check — sensitive to wording)
    - rouge_score(mode=fmeasure)   (sanity check — sensitive to wording)

A faithful, well-grounded answer typically yields green metrics
(≥ 0.80) on relevancy and faithfulness; lower BLEU/ROUGE values are expected
because the LLM paraphrases the source text. The negative/out-of-scope tests
(Q8, Q9) should still score high on faithfulness if the model correctly
refuses or qualifies its answer.
